Guest Internet Access Portal

Internet Acceptable Use Policy

Children’s National Internet guest access provides patrons with free, open and equal access to the Internet.

The Internet contains a wide variety of material and opinions. It may contain material that may be inaccurate, defamatory, illegal or offensive to some people. Users are responsible for determining that the information they access is reliable and suited to their needs.

Children’s National has no control over material residing on the Internet and specifically denies any responsibility for the accuracy or quality of information obtained there.

Privacy/confidentiality:

Children’s National cannot guarantee the confidentiality of personal information transmitted over the Internet. Users should closely guard personal information, credit card numbers, passwords, and other types of authorizations when on the Internet. Parents should instruct their children NEVER to give out personal information (name, address, phone number) online.

Children and the Internet:

Children ages 10 through 16 must have parental permission to use Internet guest access or view materials on the Internet. Children under the age of 10 may only use guest access only when accompanied by a parent or guardian. Parents of minors should be aware of the existence of material which they might consider inappropriate for their children and should educate their children to be savvy Internet users. Even though an Internet filter is active on Children’s guest Internet access network, parents should realize a filter is only partially effective and monitor their children's use of the Internet. Children’s National staff cannot determine what a parent will consider appropriate for his/her child.

Rules for Usage: Children’s National has the right to set and enforce rules concerning the use of the Internet and to change those rules at any time, without notice. The user is expected to abide by the following rules:

  1. Children’s National guest Internet access network may not be used to access chatrooms or instant messaging. Web-based email is allowable.
  2. Some material on the Internet is abusive, profane, sexually-oriented or illegal. Accessing these materials from the Children’s National guest Internet access network is inappropriate and prohibited. Examples of inappropriate conduct include but are not limited to:
    • use of the Internet for illegal purposes
    • use of the Internet to send or display sexually explicit or sexually suggestive materials
    • use of the Internet for to send unsolicited commercial material or spam
    • engaging in harassing behavior such as sending or posting slanderous, sexual, threatening or patently offensive messages.
    • activities that could interfere with or disrupt computer users, services, or equipment
  3. All communications and information accessible via the Internet should be assumed to be private property. Copyright restrictions and licensing agreements may not be violated.
  4. Children’s National uses Internet filtering software. Federal law requires that everyone age 16 and under use the filtering software. Parents may not authorize filter disabling for their children.
  5. Children’s National staff have the authority to end an Internet session when the Internet Use Policy is violated. Internet access rights may be suspended or revoked by the Children’s National staff at any time, without prior notice or hearing, for violation of the conditions of use set forth in this document. Children’s National will be the sole arbiter of what constitutes abusive conduct or violation of the Children’s National Internet Use Policy.
  6. Use of the Internet by both patrons and staff may be monitored.
  7. Children’s National reserves the right to limit the amount of time users may spend online.

In consideration for the privilege of using Children’s National Internet access, users hereby release and hold harmless Children’s National from any and all claims of any nature arising from their use, or inability to use, the Internet
